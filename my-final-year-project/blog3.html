Storage of Tigernut milk drink.

Tigernut, as good as it is has it's do's and dont's. The proper way of storing it to avoid stories that touch.


Puff puff is one of the popular snacks in Nigeria. No party is complete without the small chops (appetizers) plate and no small chops plate is complete without puff puff. That's how much Nigerians love this street snack.


Tiger nut milk is best stored in an airtight bootle in the fridge, ideally in a glass bottle but plastic still works.


As sources say that tiger nut milk doesn’t last very long (just a few days!)and spoils quickly if not preserved well. If you're wondering how tiger nut milk manages to hang around for a couple of weeks without going all funky, the magic usually comes from a little friend called refrigeration.




You see, tiger nut milk doesn't typically have a super long shelf life like store-bought milk because it's a natural product without a bunch of preservatives. But don't fret – you can totally help your homemade tiger nut milk last longer.
However, once you've whipped up your creamy goodness, pop it into the fridge ASAP. Cold temps slow down the growth of bacteria and other sneaky stuff that can make your milk go bad.


If you're all about that convenience and want your tiger nut milk to last even longer, you might want to give it a little pasteurization twist. Heating your milk to a certain temp and then quickly cooling it can help extend its shelf life. Just be sure to keep an eye on the process – you want to preserve that fresh flavor while still keeping things safe.




So there you have it – while tiger nut milk might not need a preservative superhero, a little fridge love and some pasteurization know-how can definitely help it stay tasty for a couple of weeks.
