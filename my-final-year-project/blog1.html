Vital In Reducing Blood Sugar Level.

If you want to keep your blood sugar level in check, you need to try tiger nuts. The tuber’s high fiber content is necessary for slowing the absorption of sugar in the human body. These tubers are also healthy sources of amino acid arginine that increase insulin production and sensitivity. Insulin is an essential substance in blood sugar control. Tiger nuts also hinder card-digesting enzymes’ activity, leading to less sugar absorption from the gut. The procedure mimics the mechanism applied with blood sugar lowering diabetic medicines.


Improves your digestion.

Tiger nuts supper food is ideal if you want to improve your digestion. The diet contains insoluble fiber, which adds bulk to your stools and enhances food movement through your guts without any obstacles. Tiger nuts diet is also rich in resistant starch (fiber known to feed on the bacteria on human guts), which is an essential tool in ensuring seamless and smooth digestion. Tiger nuts contain other essential enzymes such as catalases, lipases, and amylases. Together, these enzymes help break down food in the guts, thereby relieving excess gas, indigestion, and diarrhea.


Tiger Nuts Are High In Natural Nutrients.

There are three main types of cyperus esculentus, black, brown, and yellow. Each one has unique nutrients, including calcium, phosphorus, proteins, iron, vitamin C, and E, among others. They also contain antioxidants, which protect the body from aging and disease like cancer heart diseases.





Lactose-free nut milk.

Tiger nut milk is a great substitute for cow milk if you are lactose intolerant. Tiger nut milk is rich in calcium, which promotes bone health and growth. According to a study, tiger nuts are nutritionally better than other non-milk alternatives, scoring among the highest in terms of healthy fat content, protein, calcium, phosphorus, and oleic acid.
